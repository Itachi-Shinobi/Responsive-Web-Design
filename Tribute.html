A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Here's a timeline of Freddie Mercury's life:

  • 1931 — Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island
  • 1955 — Kalam moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 — Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1963 — In 1963 to 1964, he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia
  • 1965 — Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 — In 1969, Kalam received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers
  • 1981 — The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushan
  • 1992 — Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1997 — Kalam received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 — In 1998, along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 1999 — Wings of Fire: An Autobiography by A P J Abdul Kalam, Arun Tiwari; Universities Press
  • 2002 — Kalam became the 11th President of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan.
  • 2015 — He died of cardiac arrest on 27 July 2015
